A daily frustration of commuting in New York: parking

By: Diana Bravo

In the greater New York area, thousands of commuters take either the Long Island Railroad or the Metro North to work every day. Every one of those commuters needs to figure out where they leave their cars for the day.

Many local train stations provide vast parking lots for commuters. This one in Old Greenwich, CT, can accomodate about 570 cars at a time. However, parking here is a two-tiered paid system. As of 2022, parking pass costs $456. For a regular commuter, that works out to abotu $1.80 for parking every workday.

The waiting list to obtain one of these passes can take months. Meanwhile, commuters must park in the day parking lots, paying $7 every time they go in to work.

Annually, daily parkers must pay $1,750 to park for work.

However, around half of the annual parking spots are empty on any given weekday.

Meanwhile, almost all of the daily parking spots are full seven days a week.

Many towns in the area implement this parking system, leaving those who cannot obtain or afford a parking pass up front paying substantially more than their counterparts. This discrepancy in parking prices can put unnecesscary strain on commuter's budgets.
